# Voice examples (Ajay)

Rhythm anchors for review loop 2 (voice pass). Tags describe **shape**, not source. **Do not copy verbatim** into new posts.

---

## Hooks

**[hook-question]** Direct question:
> Do you know humans think in images? But we communicate in textual languages.

**[hook-reframe]** Reframe before thesis:
> Before that, let's answer — should it matter?

**[hook-confession]** Confession:
> Okay, I'll admit, the title sounds a bit too extreme. But please hear me out…

**[hook-setup]** Plain setup:
> Here's how I see this.

**[hook-alert]** Confrontation:
> Angry Post alert: Who is feeding all this crap to students and newcomers?

**[hook-unpopular]** Unpopular opinion:
> I'm going to say something unpopular. Doing more and more competitive programming is NOT going to make you a successful programmer…

**[hook-experiment]** Personal experiment:
> I experimented on something completely new domain to me — People & networking and here is my experience from doing more than hundred 1:1s since April-2020.

## Section openers (H2 / H3)

**[section-vulnerable]** Statement heading + vulnerability:
> ### People don't bite.
> For various reasons, I had fear of meeting new people. Fear of what people will think of me. I'm stupid in private what if everyone finds that out.

**[section-pivot]** Pivot to lesson:
> ### People are unique, not ill-intended.
> We engineers spend most of life talking to engineers so much that everything becomes an engineering problem to us…

**[section-career]** Career insight:
> For the first 2-3 years of my career, I hardly knew who is using the piece that I was building. So, my focus was always on building the perfect piece given to me.

**[section-split]** Two-part structure:
> This challenge can be divided into two parts: 1. Overvaluing motivation. 2. Consistency means 100%

**[section-action]** Practical pivot:
> So what to do?

## Analogies and metaphors

**[analogy-movie]** Movie → concept:
> Reminds me of a movie named 'Vantage Point'… Exactly the same applies to technical concepts. One blog or a video is not going to give you the whole truth (understanding).

**[analogy-pop]** Pop culture → programming:
> A small word like 'Oh' can mean differently in a different context and with diverse emotions (remember movie PK?). Imagine writing code with words like 'Oh'!

**[analogy-object]** Everyday object:
> Have you seen rocket firecracker? It starts at its highest speed but with every passing second, its speed decreases… We are the same. Life is not a sprint, it is a marathon.

## How to use in the pipeline

1. Read 2–3 examples closest to the post type (career, technical, personal).
2. Match **rhythm**: sentence length variety, one-line paragraphs, direct address.
3. Do **not** transplant metaphors unless they fit the topic.
4. User's reflection phrasing always overrides these examples.
